My Journey.

In 2018, I started my journey into the world of computer programming. At the very beginning I never felt like I would be able to amount to most my peer’s during a month long bootcamp offered by a French school in Fremont, CA called 42. Over the course of a month you are pushed to your limits with daily exercises that are graded by your peer’s and a grading program that is unforgiving, with tests weekly. At the end you find out if you’re accepted into the school full-time. Ultimately I failed horribly my first time, and thought that computer programming might not be for me. Two days after I receieved the email that I had not been accepted I signed up for the next one, I wasn’t going to let the fear of rejection control my life. With grit and determination I went back and after I had finished the second time I received my acceptance letter and never looked back. This was a pivatol time in my life, I really felt that if I wanted something bad enough I could get it with hard work. It’s been 17 months and I’ve learned so much, made life-long friends, and skills that I genuinely believe can change the world.
